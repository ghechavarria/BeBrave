You go over to your best friend's house expecting to do homework and play video games or watch movies. Instead, when you enter her room she turns around with tears running down her face and says, "I don't know what to do." She explains that for the past month her own friends from school have bullied her — calling her mean names, leaving her out, and spreading fake rumors about her. She looks at you with tears still in her eyes and asks, "What do I do?"

What are you going to do?

Listening without blame helps your friend feel safe. You can support her while encouraging her to talk to a trusted adult who can help stop the bullying.

Questioning why she didn't speak up sooner can make her feel blamed for the bullying. She came to you because she trusts you — kindness matters more than judgment right now.
