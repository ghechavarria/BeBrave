You wake up and get ready for school. When you put on your shirt, it feels tighter than usual. You do not have time to change, so you grab a jacket to wear over it. At lunch, you take off your jacket because you are warm. A few classmates come over, point at your shirt, and call you "fat" and "chubby." You feel ashamed and angry.

What are you going to do?

You take a deep breath, put your jacket back on, and walk to a teacher. They listen and help address the bullying. Speaking up instead of fighting back keeps you safe and shows real courage.

You punch one of your classmates and a fight breaks out. You might get in trouble too, and it does not stop the mean comments. Fighting back rarely solves bullying and can make things worse.
