You go over to your best friend's house to hang out. When you walk into her room, she is crying at her laptop. Classmates have been spreading fake rumors about her online — saying she cheated on a test and copied homework. The messages are in a group chat she was removed from, and now kids at school are treating her differently. She asks, "What do I do?"

What are you going to do?

Supporting your friend without blame helps her feel less alone. You can help her save the messages and encourage her to talk to a trusted adult who can address the online rumors.

Asking why she didn't speak up sooner can make her feel blamed for the bullying. She trusted you with something painful — kindness and teamwork matter more than judgment.
